The pyruvoyl cofactor is covalently bound to the enzyme. The protein is synthesized as a proenzyme and cleaved via self-processing at Gly23-Ser24 to yield an alpha chain (C-terminal fragment) and beta chain (N-terminal fragment), and the pyruvoyl group. Beta-alanine is required for the biosynthesis of pantothenate, in which the enzyme plays a critical regulatory role. The active site of the tetrameric enzyme is located at the interface of two subunits, with a Lysine and a Histidine from the beta chain of one subunit forming the active site with residues from the alpha chain of the adjacent subunit.
